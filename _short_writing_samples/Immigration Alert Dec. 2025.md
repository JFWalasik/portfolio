title: Immigration Alert - Dec. 2025
description: Plan ahead to mitigate diverse and rapidly implemented changes 
date: 2025-12-01
---

Immigration practitioners have become aware of substantive changes to U.S. immigration which can restrict benefits and movement. While not attributable to any single regulation or mandate, we have observed a shift in how USCIS, DOS, and CBP conduct business in a more restrictive manner. 

This has included: 
-	Increased vetting or administrative processing of visas at U.S. consulates; 
-	Enhanced scrutiny of social media;
-	New fees (advanced parole and H-1B) that make entry to the U.S. impractical for some; 
-	New requirements (e.g., electronic payments) that can limit access; 
-	Additional scrutiny (e.g., rescinding automatic EAD extensions for more vetting); and
-	Other unexpected delays and requirements, often for obscure and unlikely reasons.
